Respected Sir/Madam. With thanks to this,I am mahfujur Rahman from Bangladesh .and I’m from a very small city and culture,where people don't dream big,and usually don't support the ones,who want to exceed the limits,and break the barriers. I want to make a career in my related subject and I am ready to hard work to deserve your YGS 2020. .

Hailing from urban background from one of the metropolitan city,I got a chance through Gov.Scholarship of Indonesia to enroll in reputable educational institutions which is world class.i have attended several international Youth Camp as like UM I Camp2019 in Indonesia.I really very motivated to be a part of YGS 2020..

I`m one of those fortunate youth who received information about YGS 2020,I’m also among the ones who aim to attain this opportunity.Especially the youth of rural areas of Bangladesh do not have the access to the quality education and this drives me to get myself empower enough to be able to work for the educational reforms in my country.I`m an active social worker,community contributor,traveler,and have been involved with various volunteer forums.

I`ll be able to visit different Educational institutions to learn about China government system and its policies.This will help me in understanding different cultural perspectives,Self-development and awareness,interaction with diverse people,and broadening my social horizons. On my return,I`ll replace and implement efficient policies regarding education in my native city. If I had such opportunity, that Policy would have been to “Devise an educational Policy that promote equality in educational standards at national level, and negate disparities among people that segregate them due to the difference in their educational system.” Good and quality education is considered as one of the basic necessities of life and are also considered as the major fundamental right of every human. But Unfortunately, the students of Bangladesh are not getting same quality of education at all and the peoples are not getting right democracy facilities. I am living in the area where there are almost 5 secondary level Governmental Schools, which unfortunately do not offer quality education as compared to private schools. It is mostly because the government education system still follows obsolete procedures, weak governance and less qualified teachers. Resultantly, the students of government schools still lack the contemporary skills of learning. .
